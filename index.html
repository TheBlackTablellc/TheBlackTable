FAQs

  • How do solar panels work?

    Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) cells. When sunlight hits these cells, it knocks electrons loose from their atoms, generating a flow of electricity.

  • What are the benefits of using solar power?

    Benefits include reduced electricity bills, lower carbon footprint, energy independence, low maintenance costs, and potential increase in property value.

  • How much does it cost to install solar panels?

    The cost varies depending on factors like system size, location, and available incentives.

  • How long do solar panels last?

    Most solar panels come with a warranty of 25-30 years, but they can last much longer with proper maintenance. Their efficiency might decrease slightly over time.

  • Do solar panels work in cloudy or rainy weather?

    Yes, solar panels can still generate electricity on cloudy or rainy days, but their efficiency is reduced compared to sunny days.

  • What happens to solar power at night or during cloudy days?

    Solar panels do not produce electricity at night. To have a continuous power supply, systems often include battery storage or are connected to the grid to draw power when solar production is low.

  • How much maintenance do solar panels require?

    Solar panels require very little maintenance. Regular cleaning and periodic checks to ensure they are functioning correctly are typically sufficient.

  • Can solar panels completely power a home?

    Yes, a properly sized and installed solar panel system can generate enough electricity to power a home, especially when combined with energy-efficient appliances and practices.

  • What incentives are available for installing solar panels?

    Incentives vary by location but can include federal tax credits, state rebates, net metering policies, and other financial incentives aimed at reducing the upfront cost of solar installation.
